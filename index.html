chiaroscuro

Light is
the subject.

Her thesis at Haven of Peace Academy was titled “Exploring effects of light in portraiture.” Vermeer’s patience, Caravaggio’s drama, an African palette.

The drawing below sits in the dark, the way she first saw it. Bring your own light.

The works

swipe along the wall

She paints sound.

Violinists, cellists, sheet music torn into collages. When music enters her work the air starts to swirl, oil pastel dragged in spirals until the whole sky hums.

the swirls behind you are alive. move through them.

Every painting is two paintings.

The one underneath, roses troweled on in white impasto, and the one that blooms over it. Drag the brush across to watch this mother and child come into colour.

before after

The journey so far.

An exhibition reads left to right. A painter reads bottom to top: studies first, then colour, then a thesis, then everything after it.

first, the studies

Homework from the old masters

Charcoal heads over newspaper, anatomy borrowed from sculpture. Before the colour, she earned the structure.

then colour arrived

Skin became weather

Oil pastel taught her that a sleeping face can be blue, orange and pink at once, and read truer for it.

the thesis year

Light, argued formally

Cambridge A-level, Haven of Peace Academy. Gouache portraits mounted on black board, every choice defended in the margins.

past portraiture

The gaze widened

Rabbits in the grass, a peacock's hundred eyes. The same patience she gives a face, given to fur and feather.

now

Canvases, commissions, light

Larger canvases, oils and acrylics, work that leaves the house wrapped in brown paper. The journey continues at @0pheliarts_.

Brown paper, twine,
muddy water.

Finished work leaves her desk wrapped like a letter: kraft paper, vintage stamps, a butterfly under the knot. The desk itself stays beautifully unfinished.
